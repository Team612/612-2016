16 /**
17  * Class implements the PWM generation in the FPGA.
18  *
19  * The values supplied as arguments for PWM outputs range from -1.0 to 1.0. They
20  * are mapped
21  * to the hardware dependent values, in this case 0-2000 for the FPGA.
22  * Changes are immediately sent to the FPGA, and the update occurs at the next
23  * FPGA cycle. There is no delay.
24  *
25  * As of revision 0.1.10 of the FPGA, the FPGA interprets the 0-2000 values as
26  * follows:
27  * - 2000 = maximum pulse width
28  * - 1999 to 1001 = linear scaling from "full forward" to "center"
29  * - 1000 = center value
30  * - 999 to 2 = linear scaling from "center" to "full reverse"
31  * - 1 = minimum pulse width (currently .5ms)
32  * - 0 = disabled (i.e. PWM output is held low)
33  */

58  /**
59  * kDefaultPwmPeriod is in ms
60  *
61  * - 20ms periods (50 Hz) are the "safest" setting in that this works for all
62  * devices
63  * - 20ms periods seem to be desirable for Vex Motors
64  * - 20ms periods are the specified period for HS-322HD servos, but work
65  * reliably down
66  * to 10.0 ms; starting at about 8.5ms, the servo sometimes hums and get
67  *hot;
68  * by 5.0ms the hum is nearly continuous
69  * - 10ms periods work well for Victor 884
70  * - 5ms periods allows higher update rates for Luminary Micro Jaguar speed
71  * controllers.
72  * Due to the shipping firmware on the Jaguar, we can't run the update
73  * period less
74  * than 5.05 ms.
75  *
76  * kDefaultPwmPeriod is the 1x period (5.05 ms). In hardware, the period
77  * scaling is implemented as an
78  * output squelch to get longer periods for old devices.
79  */
